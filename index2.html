Features of the Landing Page

Landing pages are a page type used to act as essentially a home page for your website. Your landing page will likely be the first page your users visit on each of your websites, so they need to give essential information, as well as offer navigation to other pages throughout your website.

The major aspect of Landing Pages that makes them different from other page types is a function called Dominantly Linked Primary Topics. These are link buttons that will lead your users to other websites and webpages you have created. Found below are the steps to setting up your Dominantly Linked Primary Topics.

Dominantly Linked Primary Topics as seen on a finished webpage

Setting Up Dominantly Linked Primary Topics

  1. On the Pages tab, click Add Page
  2. Fill out the settings, as detailed in the Add A Page tutorial (linked below)
  3. Locate the section titled Landing Page Settings, and the tabled titled Dominantly Linked Primary Topics
  4. Here, you will see a table full of your already created websites and corresponding check boxes. By clicking any three of the check boxes, you will create a link to those three websites on your Landing Page.
  5. Select three Websites you wish to have displayed on your landing page
  6. Click save at the bottom of the page, once this, and all other settings fields have been completed

Tips:

  • If you are not seeing any options available in the Dominantly Linked Primary Topics table, please make sure you have additional websites created
  • You can go back and edit these, or other settings at any time, by locating this webpage in the Pages tab, and clicking the button that says Settings

Content Pages

When setting up your websites, a Content Page will be used to create some of your most content-heavy pages. In terms of settings, there is no difference between the general settings found on the Add a Page Tutorial, and the ones found when you click Add Content Page. However, it is important when building your page further to keep in mind the layout of the page, as shown in the PDF templates linked below.

Tip: When editing your settings, it is important to remember to set a parent page if this content page will be linked from another page that comes prior to it
